I received my early acceptance from WashU medicine on December 12, following a rigorous interview on Dec 5th. So, it was pretty much fixed that I am leaving Bangladesh for my PhD dream. And, so would most of my friends. Thus, we decided to make one last trip to the place closest to our hearts? To Bandarban? 


This was completely different from my other trips. This was the first time, we didn't have a guide. We decided to do it all on our own. Moreover, I always boasted of being a great pathfinder. So, I have my own GPS system in my brain for navigating the mountains.

<div class="travel-photo">
  <img src="/images/travel/bandarban_2024/0.jpg" alt="Travel photo from bandarban_2024">
  <p> So, as always the trip began with an around 2 hr boat journey from Thanchi to Remakri. As soon as you get on the boat, you lose any last network signal for the next few days. So, trips like these definitely are a form of digital detox for me. The Sangu river was beautiful as always with the mighty Chimbuk mountain range in distance. </p>
</div>

</div>

  <p>The return was an insane hike through a route called Padma Jhiri, and  we definitely lost our way at least 5 times, and went into places we had no clue about, but we finally made it back to Thanchi. There was a village where we stopped for snacks. Under the bench I was sitting on, this small guy was sleeping. Didn't want to wake him, but sadly I did. Just like this buddy, I was looking forward to my next journey, to WashU! </p>
